How'd I get started?

In 2010, I began to track weights as I learned the art of lifting. An eventual habit of tracking calories led to a serendipitous change in lifestyle as the number on the scale began to drop.

Serendipitously

Getting in the Groove

I became more regimented in terms of having a routine

  • Tracked lifting progress with Fitocracy
    • Gamification == level up IRL
  • Tracked biometrics with a plain ol' spreadsheet
    • Pounds
    • Body fat
    • Tape measurements
    • Clothing
  • Nutrition is #1
    • Tweaked calorie and macronutrient levels
      • Low carb, high protein, moderate fats
      • High carb, moderate protein, moderate fats
      • Macronutrient timing
  • Trends in data over time drove me to iterate on process to maintain progress

Learned my limits

Eat less, move more? …not always the best

Proceed with caution!

More != better

Low calories & heavy lifting do not play well together and not sustainable.

Tracking both elements let me see my thresholds and begin the process of reverse dieting

Moderation created

STICKY HABITS

  • Abs are made in the kitchen but you can have your ice cream too
  • Tracking helped me make lifestyle changes instead of being in a “I’m on a diet” mindset
  • Seeing numbers on the barbell go up made lifting a hobby and not a chore.
Eventually, habits will form. I climbed Half Dome a couple of weekends ago after passing up on the opportunity 5 years ago. Why did I pass it up? I had no confidence in my level of fitness.

Biggest Challenges

  • Patience
    • Results don’t happen overnight
  • Consistency
    • Can’t change too many variables at once
    • Hard to pinpoint what works and what doesn’t
