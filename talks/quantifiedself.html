Learned my limits

Eat less, move more? …not always the best

More != better
Low calories & heavy lifting do not play well together.

Proceed with caution! It’s not sustainable for a long period of time and requires reverse dieting to scale back.
Reverse dieting? Add more calories to diet until you stop losing.

Moderation creates

STICKY HABITS

  • Abs are made in the kitchen but you can have your ice cream too
  • Tracking helped me make lifestyle changes instead of being in a “I’m on a diet” mindset
  • Seeing numbers on the barbell go up made lifting a hobby and not a chore.
Eventually, habits will form. I climbed Half Dome a couple of weekends ago after passing up on the opportunity 5 years ago. Why did I pass it up? I had no confidence in my level of fitness.

Biggest Challenges

  • Patience
    • Results don’t happen overnight
  • Consistency
    • Can’t change too many variables at once
    • Hard to pinpoint what works and what doesn’t
